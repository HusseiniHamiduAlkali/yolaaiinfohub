About MAUTH Jumma'at Mosque

The MAUTH Jumma'at Mosque is a major Islamic worship center serving the Modibbo Adama University of Technology Yola (MAUTH) community and surrounding areas. The mosque plays a crucial role in providing spiritual guidance and religious services to students, staff, and the local Muslim population.

Services and Facilities

  • Five daily prayers with regular prayer schedules
  • Friday congregational prayers (Jumu'ah) with large attendance
  • Islamic lectures and religious seminars
  • Quranic study and memorization programs
  • Prayer halls accommodating large congregations
  • Clean and modern ablution facilities

University Community Focus

  • Primary mosque for MAUTH students and staff
  • Welcomes university visitors and international students
  • Facilitates interfaith understanding on campus
  • Organizes Islamic awareness events and programs
  • Provides counseling for student spiritual matters
  • Promotes Islamic values within university community

Prayer Schedule

  • Fajr (Dawn prayer) - Before sunrise
  • Dhuhr (Noon prayer) - Midday
  • Asr (Afternoon prayer) - Late afternoon
  • Maghrib (Sunset prayer) - After sunset
  • Isha (Night prayer) - Evening
  • Friday prayers with special congregation

Additional Activities

  • Islamic study groups for students
  • Eid celebrations and festivities
  • Youth development programs
  • Community social events
  • Islamic seminars and workshops
  • Women's Islamic education circles

Contact Information

Location: MAUTH Campus, Yola, Adamawa State

The mosque is conveniently located within the university campus, making it easily accessible to the academic community and nearby residents.
