Modibbo Adama Central Mosque, Yola

Historic Islamic Worship Center

About Modibbo Adama Central Mosque

The Modibbo Adama Central Mosque is one of the most significant Islamic structures in Yola, Adamawa State. It is a very large and historic mosque located at the Lamido palace, serving as the primary place of worship for the Muslim community. The mosque is renowned for its architectural grandeur and historical importance to the Islamic heritage of Yola.

Historical Significance

  • Historic landmark associated with the Lamido Palace
  • Major religious center with deep cultural roots
  • Serves as a symbol of Islamic architecture in the region
  • Important venue for major Islamic celebrations and festivals
  • Facilitates religious gatherings and community events

Facilities and Features

  • Large prayer halls accommodating hundreds of worshippers
  • Spacious courtyard for outdoor prayers
  • Traditional Islamic architectural design
  • Separate sections for male and female worshippers
  • Ablution facilities for ritual purification
  • Well-maintained grounds and surrounding areas

Religious Services

  • Five daily prayers (Salat) services
  • Friday congregational prayers (Jumu'ah)
  • Islamic lectures and Quranic recitations
  • Eid celebrations and festivities
  • Community social gatherings
  • Religious education and teachings

Contact Information

Location: Lamido Palace, Yola, Adamawa State

The mosque is centrally located near the traditional ruler's palace, making it easily accessible to the local Muslim community and visitors from other areas.
