About ABC Radio Yola

ABC Radio 102.3 Yola is a privately-owned commercial radio station with a broad mandate to serve the diverse communities of Adamawa State. Our "ABC" stands for "Adamawa Broadcast Corporation," reflecting our commitment to being a comprehensive source of information, education, and entertainment. We are a station for every household, bridging urban and rural divides through programming that is relevant, accessible, and inclusive.

Our mission is to foster a well-informed society by providing credible news, fostering public dialogue on social issues, and celebrating the rich cultural heritage of the region. We are dedicated to delivering content that not only entertains but also contributes to the personal and community development of our listeners.

Our Programming

Our daily broadcast schedule is carefully curated to offer something for every member of the family.

  • Morning Matters (6am - 9am): A fast-paced mix of local headlines, international news, and current affairs analysis, setting the agenda for the day.
  • The Civic Square (Wednesdays): A weekly interactive talk show where we invite community leaders, activists, and policymakers to discuss pressing social and civic issues.
  • Local Rhythms (Daily): A dedicated segment for local music and traditional songs, celebrating the diverse musical talent of Adamawa.
  • Education for All (Fridays): This program focuses on educational resources, from basic literacy to vocational training, and features interviews with educators and students.
  • Rural Report (Daily): A specialized segment that brings news, market prices, and agricultural advice to farmers and residents of rural communities.
