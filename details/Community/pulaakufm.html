About Pulaaku FM

Pulaaku FM 95.3 Yola is a unique radio station dedicated to the preservation and promotion of the rich culture, history, and language of the Fulani people. "Pulaaku" is the ancient code of conduct and ethics that defines Fulani identity, encompassing virtues like patience, discipline, and respect. Our station serves as a cultural repository and a modern platform for celebrating this deep heritage, connecting Fulani communities across Adamawa and beyond.

Our mission is to provide high-quality, culturally resonant programming primarily in the Fulfulde language, while also broadcasting in other local languages to foster inter-ethnic understanding. We aim to educate a new generation on the importance of their roots, from traditional music and poetry to the time-honored practices of animal husbandry and agriculture.

Our Programming

Our broadcast is a blend of traditional content and modern information, all delivered with a cultural lens.

  • Haala Pulaaku (The Fulani Talk) - Daily: A flagship program discussing the tenets of Pulaaku, including traditions, proverbs, and social customs, fostering community dialogue.
  • Wakaaji Fulani (Fulani Songs) - Daily: Featuring traditional and modern Fulfulde music, from the sounds of the Goge and Sere to contemporary artists.
  • Ngol Danye (The Cattle Path) - Weekly: A show dedicated to pastoralists, providing essential information on animal health, grazing routes, market prices, and weather forecasts.
  • Tarihi Fombina (History of Fombina) - Weekly: An in-depth historical series chronicling the history of the Adamawa Emirate, its founders, and the significant events that shaped the region.
  • Linguistics & Literature - Weekly: A segment focused on teaching the Fulfulde language and celebrating its rich oral and written literature.

Our Audience and Community Role

Pulaaku FM primarily serves the Fulani communities in Adamawa State and neighboring regions. We are a crucial source of information for both settled and nomadic populations, offering relevant news and educational content. We also serve as a bridge, helping other communities appreciate the depth of Fulani culture and promoting peaceful coexistence through cultural exchange programs and broadcasts in other local languages. We believe that understanding our past is key to building a harmonious future.
