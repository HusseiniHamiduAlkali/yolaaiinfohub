About ATV Yola

ATV, or Adamawa Television, is the official state-owned television broadcaster for Adamawa State. Our primary mandate is to serve the public interest by providing timely and accurate information on government policies, development projects, and public events. We act as a vital communication channel between the state government, traditional institutions, and the citizens, ensuring transparency and civic engagement. Our broadcasting is a mix of English and key local languages to ensure wide reach and accessibility.

Our mission is to foster a sense of shared identity and collective responsibility among the diverse people of Adamawa. We are committed to showcasing the state's potential, celebrating its unique heritage, and providing a platform for constructive dialogue on issues critical to our shared future.

Our Programs

ATV's programming is focused on public service, with a strong emphasis on news, education, and cultural promotion.

  • State House Report (Daily): A bulletin providing a detailed account of the Governor's official activities, government initiatives, and cabinet decisions.
  • Adamawa Today (Daily): A flagship news and current affairs show that features live discussions with policymakers and experts on governance and community development.
  • Our Heritage (Weekly): A cultural program that explores the traditions, history, and art of various ethnic groups in Adamawa, including the Fombina Kingdom's legacy.
  • Health Watch (Weekly): A public service program that features medical professionals discussing health issues, providing advice, and raising awareness about government health campaigns.
  • Community Connections (Saturdays): A show that visits different local government areas to highlight their unique challenges and achievements, giving a voice to grassroots communities.
