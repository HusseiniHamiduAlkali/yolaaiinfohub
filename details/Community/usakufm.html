About Usaku FM

Usaku FM 106.1 is a community-based radio station with a deep commitment to reflecting the voices and concerns of the grassroots communities of Adamawa. "Usaku," a local term for unity and collective effort, defines our core philosophy. Our station is a non-profit venture aimed at empowering local populations by providing a platform for them to share their stories, discuss local issues, and celebrate their cultural diversity. We are the radio station for the people, run by the people.

Our mission is to foster local pride and resilience by broadcasting predominantly in local languages, ensuring that our content is accessible to every community member, regardless of their level of formal education. We believe that by focusing on local news, traditional music, and community-led discussions, we can strengthen the social fabric of the entire state.

Our Programming

Our programming is designed to be deeply rooted in local life and traditions.

  • The Community Report (Daily): A program that focuses on news from the local government areas and villages, covering traditional ceremonies, market updates, and community projects.
  • Stories of Our Land (Weekly): A storytelling series that broadcasts folktales, historical accounts, and proverbs from various ethnic groups in the state, helping to preserve oral traditions.
  • Tradition Meets Today (Weekly): A talk show that discusses how traditional values can be applied to solve modern-day challenges, from justice and governance to family life.
  • Local Music Showcase (Daily): Featuring music from talented local artists, celebrating the rich and diverse sounds of Adamawa's indigenous music.
  • Farmers' Corner (Daily): A segment providing practical advice to local farmers on planting, harvesting, and livestock management, as well as providing updates on government agricultural support programs.
