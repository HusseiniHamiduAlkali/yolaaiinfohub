Yola Community Garden

Public Garden Space for Community Farming and Environmental Education

About Yola Community Garden

Yola Community Garden is a public garden space dedicated to community farming and environmental education. The garden provides a venue for residents to grow their own food, learn sustainable agriculture, and connect with their community while improving their local environment.

Garden Features

  • Vegetable Plots: Individual plots for community members
  • Herb Section: Medicinal and culinary herbs
  • Fruit Trees: Mango, cashew, and citrus trees
  • Composting Area: Waste reduction and soil enrichment
  • Educational Space: Training and demonstration areas

How to Participate

  • Plot Rental: Small plots available for community members
  • Volunteer Work: Help maintain common areas
  • Training Participation: Learn sustainable farming
  • Events: Join community gardening activities
  • Donations: Support garden improvements

Educational Programs

  • Organic Farming: Training in sustainable agriculture
  • Composting: Waste management and soil creation
  • Water Conservation: Efficient irrigation methods
  • Nutrition: Health benefits of home-grown food
  • Environmental Science: Hands-on ecology learning

Garden Rules and Guidelines

  • No chemical pesticides - organic methods only
  • Respect shared water and tool resources
  • Maintain your plot and common areas
  • Participate in community maintenance days
  • Share surplus produce with community

Benefits to Community

  • Fresh, affordable food production
  • Improved nutrition and health
  • Community building and interaction
  • Environmental education
  • Green space for recreation and relaxation
  • Mental health and well-being improvements

Membership Information

  • Plot Size: 20m² to 40m² available
  • Annual Fee: Affordable membership rates
  • Season: Year-round gardening
  • Tools: Shared equipment provided
  • Water: Irrigation system available
