American University of Nigeria (AUN)

Development University for Africa

About AUN

The American University of Nigeria (AUN) is a private university located in Yola, Adamawa State. Founded in 2003, it is Africa's first Development University. AUN offers an American-style education adapted to the needs of Africa, preparing students to be leaders in their communities and to find solutions to African development challenges.

Academic Programs

AUN offers undergraduate and graduate programs through its schools:

  • School of Business & Entrepreneurship: Accounting, Business Administration, Finance
  • School of IT & Computing: Computer Science, Software Engineering, Information Systems
  • School of Arts & Sciences: Natural & Environmental Sciences, Petroleum Chemistry
  • School of Law: LLB Program
  • Graduate Programs: MBA, MSc, and other advanced degrees

World-Class Facilities

  • Digital Library: One of the best-equipped libraries in West Africa
  • Smart Classrooms: Technology-enabled learning spaces
  • Research Centers: Including the Center for Development Studies
  • Sports Complex: Modern indoor and outdoor facilities
  • Student Housing: Modern residence halls with 24/7 security

Unique Features

  • American-style education system
  • Focus on entrepreneurship and sustainable development
  • Strong community engagement programs
  • International faculty from diverse backgrounds
  • Small class sizes for personalized attention

Admission Process

AUN's admission process includes:

  • Online application submission
  • SAT/ACT scores (for international students)
  • JAMB scores (for Nigerian students)
  • Interview process
  • English proficiency test (for non-native speakers)
