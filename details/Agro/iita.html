IITA Training for Farmers

Empowering Farmers with Modern Agricultural Knowledge

About the Training

The International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA) conducted an extensive training program for over 35,000 farmers in Yola and its surrounding areas. This initiative was designed to bridge the gap between traditional farming methods and modern, sustainable agricultural practices. The training covered various topics aimed at improving crop yield, soil health, and overall farm productivity. By equipping farmers with new skills and knowledge, the program sought to boost food security and economic prosperity in the region.

Training Modules

  • Soil Management: Techniques for soil testing, nutrient replenishment, and erosion control.
  • Pest and Disease Control: Integrated pest management strategies and safe pesticide use.
  • Improved Seed Varieties: Introduction to high-yield and climate-resilient crop varieties.
  • Post-Harvest Technology: Methods for reducing post-harvest losses and increasing product shelf life.
