ACRES AL Adamawa State Project

A Collaborative Initiative for Agricultural Development

About the Project

The ACRES AL (Adamawa State Collaborative for Rural Economic Sustainability) project is a multi-stakeholder initiative aimed at transforming the agricultural sector in Adamawa. The project focuses on creating sustainable value chains for key crops, improving market access for farmers, and enhancing the overall resilience of rural communities. It brings together government agencies, non-governmental organizations, and private sector partners to implement a comprehensive strategy that addresses the systemic challenges in the state's agricultural landscape.

Project Components

  • Value Chain Development: Focusing on crops like rice, maize, and cassava to link farmers with processors and markets.
  • Rural Finance Inclusion: Facilitating access to credit and savings for smallholder farmers.
  • Infrastructure Support: Assisting in the development of rural roads and storage facilities.
  • Climate-Smart Agriculture: Promoting practices that help farmers adapt to changing climate conditions.
