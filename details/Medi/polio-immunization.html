Polio Immunization Drive

A Public Health Initiative at Community Primary Healthcare Centers

About the Campaign

The polio immunization campaign is a critical public health initiative aimed at eradicating polio from our communities. This effort involves the mobilization of health workers and volunteers to administer the oral polio vaccine (OPV) to all children under five years of age. The campaign is carried out at various community Primary Healthcare Centers (PHCCs) and through door-to-door visits to ensure no child is left behind. This collective effort is vital for building a healthier, polio-free future for our children.

Campaign Objectives

  • To ensure 100% vaccination coverage for children under five.
  • To raise awareness about the importance of routine immunization.
  • To provide accurate information and dispel myths about the polio vaccine.
  • To track and monitor progress toward a polio-free Adamawa State.
