Mosquito Nets Distribution

Combating Malaria in Yola through Preventive Measures

About the Initiative

This community health initiative focuses on the mass distribution of Long-Lasting Insecticidal Nets (LLINs) to households across Yola. Malaria remains a significant health challenge in the region, and the use of treated mosquito nets is one of the most effective and low-cost ways to prevent infection. This campaign is a collaborative effort between the government, local health agencies, and international partners to reduce malaria-related morbidity and mortality, especially among children and pregnant women.

Program Goals

  • To achieve high household ownership and use of treated mosquito nets.
  • To provide comprehensive education on the proper use and maintenance of nets.
  • To reduce the incidence of malaria cases within the community.
  • To empower families to take an active role in their health and well-being.
