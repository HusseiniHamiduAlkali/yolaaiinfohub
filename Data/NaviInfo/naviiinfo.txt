General Information:

Yola is the capital of Adamawa State, Nigeria, and is located within the Yola South Local Government Area.

Yola comprises the old town of Yola and the new city of Jimeta, which is the administrative and commercial centre.

Navigation and Transportation:

- Yola is served by major roads including Lamido Zubairu Way, Modibbo Adama Way, and Dr. Mahmud Tukur Road.
- The Yola International Airport is located along Airport Road, about 10 km from the city center.
- Public transport options include taxis, motorcycle taxis (okada), tricycles (keke), and buses.
- Major motor parks: Jimeta Motor Park, Jambutu Motor Park, and Ngurore Motor Park.
- River transport is available along the Benue River for some communities.

FAQs (NaviInfo Section)

Q: How do I get from Yola Airport to Jimeta?
A: Taxis and tricycles are available at the airport. The trip takes about 20 minutes by road.

Q: What are the main motor parks in Yola?
A: Jimeta Motor Park, Jambutu Motor Park, and Ngurore Motor Park are the main hubs for intercity and local transport.

Q: Are there ride-hailing services in Yola?
A: Ride-hailing apps are not widely available, but local taxis and keke are easy to find.

Q: How can I travel to neighboring towns?
A: Use motor parks for buses and taxis to destinations like Mubi, Numan, Girei, and others.

Q: Is there river transport in Yola?
A: Yes, some communities use boats along the Benue River, especially during the rainy season.
